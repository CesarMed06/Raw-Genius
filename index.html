Naming those who made history

This website highlights individuals who, for sexist, racist, or homophobic reasons, were rendered invisible despite their essential contributions to science and technology. Through informative cards, we seek to recognise their work, rescue their stories, and give them the place they always deserved.

Rosalind Franklin
She was a British chemist and crystallographer who played a key role in understanding the structure of DNA and other molecules.

Rosalind Franklin

Biophysicist whose Photo 51 was crucial to discovering the double helix structure of DNA. Her contributions were overlooked until after her death.

Alan Turing

Katherine Johnson
She calculated the trajectory of Alan Shepard's space flight in May 1961, the first American in space.

Katherine Johnson

NASA mathematician whose calculations were critical to the success of early space missions, including the first American in space and the Apollo moon landing.

Lise Meitner
She was an Austrian scientist who contributed to the discovery of protactinium and nuclear fission.

Lise Meitner

Physicist who played a key role in the discovery of nuclear fission. Despite her crucial contributions, she was overlooked for the Nobel Prize awarded to her colleague.
